# Customer Segmentation Using K-Means Clustering

## Project Overview
This project aims to analyze customer purchasing behavior using clustering techniques. By applying the K-Means clustering algorithm, customers are grouped into different segments based on demographic characteristics and spending behavior. The insights from these segments can help businesses develop more effective marketing strategies and improve sales performance.

## Dataset
The dataset contains purchasing information for 200 mall customers. The features include:

- **CustomerID** – Unique identifier for each customer
- **Gender** – Customer gender
- **Age Category** – Age group category
- **Age** – Customer age
- **Annual Income (k$)** – Annual income in thousand dollars
- **Spending Score (1–100)** – Score assigned based on customer spending behavior

## Objectives
- Explore and understand customer purchasing patterns.
- Apply K-Means clustering to group customers into segments.
- Identify meaningful customer groups for targeted marketing strategies.

## Methodology
The K-Means clustering algorithm is used to segment customers based on their income and spending score. The optimal number of clusters is determined and visualized to understand customer distribution.

## Customer Segmentation Results

The clustering analysis grouped customers into five segments:

- **Cluster 1 (Blue):** High income and high spending customers (target customers).
- **Cluster 2 (Yellow):** Low income and low spending customers.
- **Cluster 3 (Green):** Average income and average spending customers.
- **Cluster 4 (Red):** High income but low spending customers.
- **Cluster 5 (Purple):** Low income but high spending customers.

These segments help the mall understand customer behavior and design appropriate marketing strategies for each group.

## Results
The clustering visualization clearly shows distinct customer groups, allowing the business to identify target customers and optimize marketing strategies for different customer segments.
